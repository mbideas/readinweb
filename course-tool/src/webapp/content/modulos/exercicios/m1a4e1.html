I use an old version of Wordperfect on my PC. I have mastered many of the basic features and periodically check the almost useless "help" function. I have never opened the manual. The one thing I do not need is a new version of Wordperfect - or of any of the other software on my machine, for that matter. I am not a troglodyte. I ride the Third Wave. But here is my admonition to consumers: do not get the new version if the old one is working O.K. In my work in companies large and small, I have witnessed one constant theme - the great majority of people use a very small portion of the power and productivity available from the computer. For many businesses, the rule should be: stop buying new software, and train employees on what you have. Does anyone ask the users if they want new versions? Do companies ask anyone besides internal consultants and systems directors? Hardly unbiased sources. Many software companies will not appreciate this advice because their business is selling new products, not unlike the auto industry that plans obsolescence.
